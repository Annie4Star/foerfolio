Product Photography

Conrads College Gifts

May 2015 to December 2019 (Projected)

Most of my growth as an image editor comes from my time at Conrads College Gifts. I used Photoshop for some editing and digital painting up to this point. However, it was at Conrads I got to apply my learning in a real world setting, and establish myself as a stickler for quality. I did a lot of reading in my own time to take the best pictures possible. This included white backdrops and dark sides on glass, lighting principles, and more. I strove for color correction (as many of my peers did not) and getting good angles. As the site involved I was able to experiment with multiple images of the same product. Later on, I also made simulated images of products in use or at home.

Online Marketplaces

This is mostly personal photography for online marketplace postings. I applied a lot of what I learned at Conrads and online articles. I made a lightbox specifically for this purpose of an old cardboard box and muslin. I feel the lightbox really brought out the texture of the glass and ceramics I was working with.

Photo Restoration

My mom found an old yearbook photo of her from between 1959 and 1962. It had seen some damage over the years, and asked if I could fix the tearing in the middle. I was supplied with a smaller colorless copy of the same photo for help around the nose. I could not be satsfied with just fixing the damage, so I also did some retouching. I brought out the colors, fixed the yellowing, and increased the contrast.

I would like to go through more fmaily photos when I have a chance to do more retouching.

A side by side comparison of the torn and faded (before) image and my restoration and retouching (after) image.
