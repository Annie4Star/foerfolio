Web Design

July 2015 to December 2019 - Drupal 7 Commerce, WAMP, and Notepad++

The Conrads College Gifts website was inefficient and dated when I arrived. In addition, file uploading was divided between multiple platforms, which required downgrading the computer's version of Explorer.
My contribution was the development of a custom Drupal theme, from a PSD, with the use of HTML and CSS3. I also assisted with making the website responsive. The official publish date was in February 2017.

Product Photography

May 2015 to December 2019 - Point & shoot camera, Adobe Photoshop CS6, and Microsoft Access

Online product listings comprised the bulk of my day to day duties. There was not a dedicated staff member when I arrived at Conrads College Gifts. Images were of poor quality; taken at poor angles, with inconsistent color matching. Product descriptions were rushed, poorly edited, and vague.
During my tenure I implemented strict guidelines for images. Matching color, unbroken but removed packaging, wrinkle and spot removal, and more. I worked to improve the quality of written descriptions. I did so by including care instructions and involving all applicable human senses.

Graphic Design

August 2015 to December 2019 - Adobe Photoshop and InDesign CS6

A common design task we had was a product featurette and ad for a local publication called the Buckeye Sports Bulletin, BSB. I worked from a premade full page and 4 color template. I modified this template to clean up and organize layers.
Other projects included bi-monthly product features, publication ads, and social media content. I worked with the manager to have weekly to daily content. Our collaboration made us the most followed local OSU store on Instagram.

Flairdocs/Flairsoft

July 2014, Photoshop and InDesign CS3

Flairdocs was headed to a business conference in about a week's time, and needed a designer as their original person was unreachable. Someone within the company was a former schoolmate, and he reached out to me to make a set of flyers. They were completed in time for mass printing the day before the conference.
The CEO wanted a single, one-sided page each of three upcoming programs he wanted to introduce at the show. I made the layout in InDesign, did some complementary cleanup on provided images, added provided text in a legible manner, and pre-flighted the flyers for print.
